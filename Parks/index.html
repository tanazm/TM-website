Disparities in accessing Atlanta's green spaces

Atlanta’s 190 regional parks, neighborhood parks, community parks, playlots, nature preserves, plazas, and trails are unevenly distributed across its twelve City Council Districts.

Districts 10, 11, and 12 – where approximately 90 percent or more of residents are Black – have the fewest number of green spaces while majority-white Districts – such as District 6 – have the greatest number of green spaces.

Access to public green spaces is linked to a myriad of physical and psychological benefits. One review of 143 studies concluded that access to green spaces is associated with reduced incidence of strokes, hypertension, asthma, and coronary heart disease. Other studies have linked exposure to green spaces with increased happiness and a reduction in perceived stress.

But despite being a majority-Black city, Atlanta’s green spaces favor its white residents. Districts 10, 11, and 12 are home to only eleven parks each despite being larger in acreage than the majority of City Council Districts.
